The Structural Premise

Most restaurant projects begin with execution. Logos. Menus. Websites. Interior concepts.

But execution without structural clarity creates expensive revisions later — operational, financial, and brand-level.

Decision Architecture separates structural alignment from design execution.

Decision Architecture

Decision Architecture examines the structure behind a restaurant concept before design begins.

This stage clarifies:

  • Revenue model clarity
  • Offer hierarchy and positioning
  • Customer flow (digital and physical)
  • Operational constraints
  • Brand positioning aligned with real capacity

The outcome is a structural map for the concept.

If misalignment is detected, execution does not begin.

This protects capital before larger expenditures occur.

Design Translation

Once the structure is verified, design becomes translation.

Identity systems, web architecture, menu structures, environmental graphics, and digital touchpoints are executed according to the verified structure.

Implementation follows verified structure. Not the other way around.

How Engagement Works

Engagement begins with Decision Architecture.

Only after structural alignment is verified does Design Translation begin.

Both stages remain under one accountability.
